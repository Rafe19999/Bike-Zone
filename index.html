Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

You should replace your helmet every 5 years or after any significant impact, even if there's no visible damage. Check for signs like cracks, dents, worn-out padding, or a loose fit as indicators for replacement.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding a bike that’s been in storage, inspect the tires for cracks or low pressure, check the brakes, and ensure the chain is lubricated. It's also a good idea to test the battery, lights, and fluids for safety and performance.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding your bike, follow these key rules:

  • 1. Always wear a helmet and protective gear.
  • 2. Obey traffic laws, signals, and signs.
  • 3. Stay visible by using lights and reflective clothing.
  • 4. Ride in designated bike lanes when available, and signal before turning or changing lanes.
  • 5. Keep a safe distance from vehicles and avoid distractions.
